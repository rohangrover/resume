About Me

Hi, I'm Rohan! I'm a doctoral student at the Annenberg School for Communication and Journalism at USC.

I study how technology policy travels across space and across actors. I am specifically interested in understanding how privacy regulations, such as the GDPR and CCPA, have been (mis)translated as they are passed along from activists to policymakers to software engineers to regulators who bring different geopolitical dispositions to shape what "privacy" means around the world.

I approach this topic by drawing from scholarship on internet governance, science and technology studies (STS), critical data studies, and decolonial politics. I also have secondary interests in platform studies, political communication, and journalism.

My work has been published in Telecommunications Policy; Information, Communication & Society; and the Asian American Policy Review. My research has been supported by APSA, AEJMC, the Center on Science, Technology, and Public Life, the Asian/Pacific/American Institute, and the Membership Puzzle Project.

I have a Master of Arts in Media, Culture, and Communication from NYU and a Bachelor of Science in Economics, with a minor in Asian American Studies, from the Wharton School at the University of Pennsylvania.

Before beginning the PhD, I worked as a product manager, data analyst, and digital strategist in progressive digital media and advocacy organizations such as MoveOn, HuffPost, Upworthy, and Planned Parenthood.
